2.1 Usage Rules
Sentence case always — headings, buttons, labels
No periods at the end of headings or button labels
No highlighted italic text (brand rule)
No ALL CAPS for emphasis — use SemiBold weight instead
Maximum one exclamation mark per document or deck
Fallbacks: Georgia for Darker Grotesque, Arial for Poppins, Epilogue also accepted for headings

3.1 Layout & Spacing Principles
Generous whitespace — 'less cluttered, simplified' is a core design direction
Align content to a clear grid — don't fill every corner
Breathing room signals confidence — a sparse slide is intentional
Accent bars: bold horizontal bar at bottom of dark slides using gradient #55F366 → #1FE7DC
Corner badge: circle or pill in #55F366 green with 'ii' or other mark — top-right position
Content slides: left-align text blocks, right-side imagery or data visualisation
3.2 Shapes & Graphic Elements
Use
Flat geometric fills — no drop shadows
Rounded rectangles (6–12pt corner radius)
Bold accent bars at slide bottom or left edge
Circle / pill badges in brand green (#55F366) or teal (#1FE7DC)
Simple flat icon style — supports a point, never leads
Data visualisations with brand colour fills
Avoid
Drop shadows or depth effects on shapes
Heavy gradients across large shape areas
Old house/sun icon
Old pizza-box or legacy illustrations
Highlighted italic text anywhere
Overly complex or decorative illustrations
4. Imagery Guidelines
Photography and imagery reinforce the brand's warm-but-tech feel. All images should feel real and relatable, not generic or overly corporate.
Use
Realistic, relatable lifestyle scenarios — people in homes, family moments
Warm-neutral colour tone, leaning toward eggshell warmth
Include green tones — in the original shot or colour-corrected in post
Images that show the idea being communicated (not decorative filler)
Natural light, unposed-feeling compositions
Avoid
Generic stock imagery — people in suits shaking hands
Obviously AI-generated faces (uncanny valley, too-warm tones)
Overly warm or orange tones without green counterbalance
Photos unrelated to the slide narrative
Old brand illustrations (pizza box, house/sun icon)
Colour correction tip: if a stock image is too warm and lacks green, colour-grade it in Photoshop to add a slight cool-green shift. Reference the Tiimely Home brand book for before/after examples.
5. Tone of Voice
Personable Professional
Our tone of voice closely follows our values. We speak like a switched-on mortgage expert who explains home loans in plain language and never hides the fine print. We blend a tech-first approach with excellent human support.
5.1 Four Core Principles
Tech-first, human-backed  — Emphasise our technology advantage while reassuring that human support is always available.
Confident, not salesy  — Don't over-hype. Back claims up with specific figures.
Relatable, not quirky  — Not a stuffy corporate, not a startup trying to be edgy. Professionals providing a personable experience.
Clear and specific  — Short sentences, plain language, active voice. Always with substance — never write to fill space.
5.2 Writing Rules
Do
Active, conversational sentences — "You can do the whole application on your phone"
Use contractions — "you're" not "you are"
Specific numbers — "approved in as little as 58 minutes" not "quickly"
Front-load benefits, then explain features
Sentence case headlines, no periods
Max one exclamation mark per document
Clear, actionable CTAs — "Buy", "Refinance", "Talk to a mortgage broker"
Don't
Heavy jargon without a quick explanation
Over-claiming speed, abilities or rates without backing data
Hiding fees or conditions — list them plainly
"brokers" — always say "mortgage brokers"
Highlighted italic text
ALL CAPS for emphasis — use bold weight instead
Generic filler phrases — "world-class", "seamless", "cutting-edge" without evidence
5.3 Audience Tone Calibration
Tone can flex slightly depending on audience, while staying within the personable professional range:

Tone

6. Brand Identifiers
6.1 The Double 'ii'
The double 'ii' in Tiimely is a core brand signal — not just a spelling quirk. It represents the "human in the loop" signature process and signals that Tiimely's technology and teams are both intelligent and intuitive.
In design, the 'ii' can appear as a decorative badge, corner mark, or watermark element — typically in a circle or pill shape using brand green (#55F366) with black text on dark slides, or black (#000404) with green text on light slides.

7. LLM Prompt Cheatsheet
Copy the block below into any LLM prompt to instruct it to produce Tiimely-branded output. Adjust the final instruction to describe your specific deliverable.
PROMPT BLOCK — copy from here:
Use the Tiimely brand design system throughout this output.
COLOURS:
Primary: #000404 (Tiimely Black), #55F366 (Tiimely Green), #1FE7DC (Teal)
Backgrounds: #000404 dark hero | #FBF9F3 eggshell content | #FFFFFF clean white | #55F366 green emphasis
Retail accents: #75FAAB Minty Fresh | #81ECF5 Sky Blue | #FCFE7F Sunshine
On dark backgrounds use 20% tints: #E5FEEE | #E7FBFE | #FEFFE7
Gradients (text highlights only): #000404 → #357762 (dark) or #55F366 → #1FE7DC (green)
TYPOGRAPHY:
Headlines (H1-H3): Darker Grotesque Black/900 (fallback: Georgia). Tight leading 1.1-1.2.
Sub-headings (H4-H6): Poppins Bold/700
Body: Poppins Regular/400, line-height 1.6
Highlight: Poppins SemiBold/600
Labels/eyebrows: Poppins Medium/500, UPPERCASE, letter-spaced
ALWAYS sentence case. No periods on headings. Never ALL CAPS for emphasis.
LAYOUT:
Generous whitespace. Flat geometric shapes. No drop shadows.
Rounded corners (6-12pt). Accent bars using gradient at slide bottom.
Circle/pill badges with 'ii' mark in brand green.
IMAGERY:
Realistic lifestyle photography with warm-neutral, slightly green colour grading.
No generic stock. No AI-generated faces. Include green tones.
TONE OF VOICE: Personable Professional.
Human, transparent, jargon-free. Tech-first, human-backed.
Confident (not salesy). Specific numbers. Active voice. Contractions OK.
Max one exclamation mark. Say 'mortgage brokers' not 'brokers'.
Now please [DESCRIBE YOUR DELIVERABLE HERE].
